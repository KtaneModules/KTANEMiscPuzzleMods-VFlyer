On the Subject of Sploosh Kaboom

You are 30 minutes on a world record pace. All that stands between you and this record is this RNG nightmare.

This module consists of an 8x8 board and a display on the left denoting how many shots you have used so far.

To disarm this module, win a game of Sploosh Kaboom at least twice, and have at least one win use 19 shots or fewer.

The rules of Sploosh Kaboom are the following:

  • You are given 24 shots to find 3 squids.
  • The three squids each are 2, 3, and 4 tiles long. There will never be a duplicate squid of that length.
  • Squids may touch each other orthogonally, or diagonally. The squids will NEVER overlap.

Failing to find the 3 squids will reveal their positions. Tapping the display will reset the module with a new board, unless all shots have not been used yet, the module will consume rest of the shots by shooting randomly.

Holding the display for at least 2 seconds and then releasing will HARD reset the module, by resetting the RNG, and the progress made.

This module uses a pseudo-randomizer known as Wicemann-Hill. This pseudo-randomizer is always initially called at least 100 times and no more than 300 times per module. When generating a board, 3 calls are made for each squid in the snippet of code provided, 1 to determine if the squid should be placed horizontally or vertically, and 2 for the coordinate of the squid placed. If the result of this causes overlapping squids, 3 more of the mentioned calls are made until the squid placed no longer overlaps.

#Seed the state

# Wichmann-Hill Generator
def rng():
s1 = 171 * s1 % 30269
s2 = 172 * s2 % 30307
s3 = 170 * s3 % 30323
return (s1 / 30269 + s2 / 30307 + s3 / 30323) % 1.0
